What is a Solar Eclipse?

Before we dive into the details of the solar eclipse map in Oregon, let’s first take a closer look at what a solar eclipse actually is. A solar eclipse occurs when the moon passes between the sun and Earth and blocks the sun’s light. There are three types of solar eclipses: total, partial, and annular. A total solar eclipse is the rarest and most spectacular type of eclipse, occurring when the moon completely blocks the sun, creating a dark shadow on the Earth’s surface.

Where Can You See the Solar Eclipse in Oregon?

The path of totality will stretch across the state from the west coast to the east coast, passing through cities such as Lincoln City, Salem, Albany, Madras, and Baker City. If you want to see the total solar eclipse, it’s important to be in the path of totality. However, even if you’re not in the path of totality, you’ll still be able to see a partial solar eclipse.

What Should You Bring to Watch the Solar Eclipse?

If you’re planning to watch the solar eclipse in Oregon, there are a few things you should bring with you. What Should You Expect During the Solar Eclipse?

During the solar eclipse, you’ll experience a few unique phenomena. As the moon passes in front of the sun, the sky will gradually darken, and the temperature will drop.
