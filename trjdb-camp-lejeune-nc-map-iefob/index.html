    The History and Significance of Camp Lejeune

    Located in Jacksonville, North Carolina, Camp Lejeune is one of the largest Marine Corps bases in the United States. Established in 1941, the base was named after Lieutenant General John A. Lejeune, the 13th Commandant of the Marine Corps. Over the years, the base has played a significant role in training and deploying troops for various operations around the world.
