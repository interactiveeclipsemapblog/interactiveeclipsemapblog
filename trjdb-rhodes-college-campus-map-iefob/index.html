Introduction

Rhodes College is a private liberal arts college located in Memphis, Tennessee. With a beautiful campus that spans over 100 acres, it can be a challenge for new students and visitors to navigate their way around. This article aims to provide a comprehensive guide to the Rhodes College campus map, including tips and tricks on how to find your way around.

Campus Layout

The Rhodes College campus is divided into two main areas: the academic quad and the residential quad. The academic quad is located in the northern part of the campus and includes buildings such as the Burrow Library, the Briggs Student Center, and the Buckman Hall. The residential quad, on the other hand, is located in the southern part of the campus and includes dormitories and residential buildings.

Academic Quad

The academic quad is where most of the classrooms and academic buildings are located. The Burrow Library is one of the most important buildings on the academic quad as it serves as the main research library for the college. The Briggs Student Center is also located here and serves as a hub for student activities and organizations. Buckman Hall is where most of the administrative offices are located, including the office of the president and the registrar’s office.

Residential Quad

The residential quad is where most of the dormitories and residential buildings are located. The most important building here is the Rollow Hall, which serves as the main dining hall for students. The Catherine Burrow Refectory is also located here and serves as a secondary dining hall. Other important buildings on the residential quad include Coates Hall and East Village.

Tips and Tricks

Navigating the Rhodes College campus can be a challenge, especially for new students and visitors. Here are some tips and tricks to help you find your way around:

Use the Rhodes College Campus Map

The Rhodes College campus map is a great tool to help you navigate your way around the campus. You can find it on the Rhodes College website or pick up a physical copy at the Burrow Library or Briggs Student Center.

Ask for Directions

Don’t be afraid to ask for directions if you’re lost. The students, faculty, and staff at Rhodes College are friendly and willing to help.

Attend Orientation

Attending orientation is a great way to get familiar with the Rhodes College campus. You’ll get to meet other new students, learn about the different buildings and resources on campus, and participate in fun activities.
