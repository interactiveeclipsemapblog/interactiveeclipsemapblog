    What are Zip Codes?

    Zip codes are a system of postal codes used in the United States. They were introduced in 1963 by the United States Postal Service (USPS) to improve the efficiency of mail delivery. A zip code is a series of numbers that identifies a specific geographic area, such as a city, town, or neighborhood.

    How are Zip Codes Determined?

    Zip codes are determined based on the geographic location of the area they represent. The USPS uses a system of sorting machines and scanners to read the zip codes on mail and sort it accordingly. The first digit of a zip code represents a broad geographic region, while the following digits narrow down the location to a specific area.
