What Caused the Fire?

The Kagel Canyon Fire was caused by a combination of factors, including high winds, low humidity, and an abundance of dry vegetation. Preparing for Future Fires

While the Kagel Canyon Fire was devastating, it is important to remember that wildfires are a natural part of the ecosystem in Southern California. As such, it is important to be prepared for future fires. Here are some tips:

Clear Your Property

One of the best ways to protect your home from wildfires is to clear your property of dry vegetation. This includes dead trees, shrubs, and grass. You should also make sure that your gutters are free of debris, as this can be a fire hazard.

Have an Evacuation Plan

If you live in an area that is at risk of wildfires, it is important to have an evacuation plan in place. This should include a designated meeting place, a list of important phone numbers, and a plan for your pets.

Stay Informed

Finally, it is important to stay informed about wildfires in your area. This means paying attention to the news, signing up for emergency alerts, and following local fire departments on social media.
