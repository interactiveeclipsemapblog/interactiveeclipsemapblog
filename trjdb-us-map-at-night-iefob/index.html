The Science Behind the US Map at Night

The US map at night is not just a pretty picture; it is also a testament to the progress of modern technology. The map is created from satellite images taken by the Suomi National Polar-orbiting Partnership (NPP) satellite. The satellite captures images of the Earth’s surface at night, using a special instrument called the Visible Infrared Imaging Radiometer Suite (VIIRS). The VIIRS detects the light emitted by cities, towns, and other human settlements, creating a map of the lights visible from space.

FAQs about the US Map at Night

What is the Suomi NPP satellite?

The Suomi National Polar-orbiting Partnership (NPP) satellite is a joint venture between NASA and the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA). It is designed to monitor the Earth’s climate and weather patterns, and to provide data on natural disasters such as hurricanes and wildfires.

What is light pollution?

Light pollution is the excessive use of artificial light, which can have a negative impact on the environment. It can disrupt the natural habitat of wildlife, affect plant growth, and interfere with our ability to see the night sky.

What is the Dark Skies program?

The Dark Skies program is a National Park Service initiative designed to reduce light pollution and preserve the natural beauty of the night sky. It aims to educate the public about the importance of dark skies and to promote responsible lighting practices.
