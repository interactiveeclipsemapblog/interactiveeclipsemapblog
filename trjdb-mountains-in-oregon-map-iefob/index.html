Discovering Oregon’s Mountains

Oregon is a state that is well-known for its stunning landscapes and breathtaking mountains. With over 360 peaks that stand over 1,000 meters tall, Oregon is a paradise for hikers, climbers, and nature enthusiasts. The state is home to several mountain ranges, including the Cascade Range, the Blue Mountains, and the Wallowa Mountains. Each of these ranges offers a unique experience and awe-inspiring scenery that is worth exploring.

The Cascade Range

The Cascade Range is the most famous mountain range in Oregon, and it stretches from British Columbia in Canada to Northern California. This range is known for its active volcanoes, including Mount Hood, Mount Jefferson, and Mount Bachelor. The Cascade Range has over 20 peaks that are over 3,000 meters tall, making it a popular destination for climbers and hikers.

The Blue Mountains

The Blue Mountains are located in Eastern Oregon and are part of the Rocky Mountains. This range is known for its vast forests, stunning landscapes, and abundant wildlife. The highest peak in the Blue Mountains is the Strawberry Mountain, which stands at 2,919 meters. The Blue Mountains are a popular destination for hiking, hunting, fishing, and camping.

The Wallowa Mountains

The Wallowa Mountains are located in Northeastern Oregon and are known as the “Alps of Oregon.” This range is home to several peaks, including the Eagle Cap, which stands at 3,169 meters. The Wallowa Mountains are a popular destination for hikers, skiers, and climbers. The range is also home to the Hells Canyon, which is the deepest river gorge in North America.
