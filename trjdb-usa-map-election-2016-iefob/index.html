The 2016 Presidential Election

The 2016 United States Presidential Election was one of the most contentious and polarizing elections in recent history. The election was held on November 8, 2016, and was between the Republican nominee, Donald Trump, and the Democratic nominee, Hillary Clinton.

The election was closely watched by the entire world, and the results were shocking. Despite losing the popular vote by nearly 3 million votes, Trump won the election by winning key battleground states. The election was marked by controversy and allegations of Russian interference.

The Electoral College

The United States Presidential Election is decided by the Electoral College, a group of 538 electors who cast their votes for the President and Vice President. Each state is allocated a certain number of electors based on their population.

In the 2016 election, Trump won 306 electoral votes, while Clinton won 232. This was a surprising result, as many pollsters had predicted a Clinton victory. The election highlighted the flaws in the Electoral College system, as Clinton won the popular vote but still lost the election.

The United States Map

The United States is divided into 50 states, each with its own unique culture, economy, and political landscape. During the election, the candidates spent a significant amount of time campaigning in key battleground states that would ultimately determine the election.

Some of the key battleground states included Florida, Pennsylvania, Michigan, and Ohio. These states were crucial to both candidates, and their victory in these states would determine the outcome of the election.

The Impact of the Election

The 2016 Presidential Election had a profound impact on the United States and the world. Trump’s victory signaled a shift in American politics, with a rise in nationalism and populism.

The election also highlighted the deep divisions within the United States, with many people feeling left behind by globalization and the changing economy. The election led to protests and demonstrations across the country, with many people feeling that their voices were not being heard.

The Future of American Politics

The 2016 Presidential Election has set the stage for future elections and the direction of American politics. The election showed that anything is possible, and that traditional political norms can be upended.
