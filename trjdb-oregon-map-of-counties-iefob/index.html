Oregon Map Of Counties: A Comprehensive Guide In 2023

admin February 9, 2023 No Comments

If you’re planning a trip to Oregon or just want to learn more about the state, having a good understanding of the counties is essential. Oregon is divided into 36 counties, each with their own unique features and attractions. In this article, we’ll take a closer look at the Oregon map of counties and explore what each county has to offer.

What Are the Counties in Oregon?

Oregon is divided into 36 counties, which are grouped into three regions: Western Oregon, Central Oregon, and Eastern Oregon. The largest county by population is Multnomah, which includes the city of Portland, while the largest county by area is Harney, which covers over 10,000 square miles.

What Are the Major Cities in Each County?

Each county in Oregon has its own unique attractions and features. Some of the major cities in each county include:

  • Multnomah: Portland
  • Washington: Hillsboro, Beaverton
  • Clackamas: Oregon City, Lake Oswego
  • Marion: Salem, Woodburn
  • Polk: Dallas, Independence
  • Linn: Albany, Lebanon
  • Benton: Corvallis
  • Lincoln: Newport, Lincoln City
  • Deschutes: Bend, Redmond
  • Jefferson: Madras
  • Crook: Prineville
  • Morrow: Boardman
  • Umatilla: Pendleton, Hermiston
  • Wallowa: Enterprise, Joseph
  • Union: La Grande
  • Baker: Baker City
  • Grant: John Day
  • Harney: Burns

What Are the Top Attractions in Each County?

There are endless things to see and do in Oregon, and each county has its own unique attractions. Here are some of the top attractions in each county:

  • Multnomah: Powell’s City of Books, Portland Japanese Garden
  • Washington: Tualatin Hills Nature Park, Baggenstos Farm
  • Clackamas: Mount Hood National Forest, Timberline Lodge
  • Marion: Silver Falls State Park, Oregon State Capitol
  • Polk: Baskett Slough National Wildlife Refuge, Dallas Aquatic Center
  • Linn: Oregon Jamboree, Willamette National Forest
  • Benton: Peavy Arboretum, Corvallis Farmers’ Market
  • Lincoln: Devils Lake State Recreation Area, Oregon Coast Aquarium
  • Deschutes: Newberry National Volcanic Monument, Pilot Butte State Park
  • Jefferson: Lake Billy Chinook, Jefferson County Historical Museum
  • Crook: Smith Rock State Park, Bowman Museum
  • Morrow: Boardman State Scenic Corridor, SAGE Center
  • Umatilla: Hat Rock State Park, Pendleton Woolen Mills
  • Wallowa: Wallowa Lake State Park, Joseph Branch Railriders
  • Union: Catherine Creek State Park, Hot Lake Springs
  • Baker: Sumpter Valley Railroad, Anthony Lakes Mountain Resort
  • Grant: Strawberry Mountain Wilderness, John Day Fossil Beds National Monument
  • Harney: Malheur National Wildlife Refuge, Steens Mountain Wilderness

Conclusion

Whether you’re planning a trip to Oregon or just want to learn more about the state, understanding the map of counties is essential. Each county has its own unique features and attractions, so be sure to explore as much of the state as possible. We hope this guide has helped you gain a better understanding of the Oregon map of counties and what each county has to offer. Happy exploring!
