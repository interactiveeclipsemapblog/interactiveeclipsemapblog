Each region has its unique culture, traditions, and landscapes. In this article, we will explore the map of France regional and discover the hidden gems of this beautiful country.

The map of France regional is essential for travelers who want to experience the diversity of France. The Regions of France

France is divided into 18 regions, which are further divided into 101 departments. The regions are:

1. Grand Est

Located in the northeast of France, Grand Est is known for its beautiful countryside, historic cities, and world-class museums. Some of the popular destinations in Grand Est are Strasbourg, Nancy, and Metz.

2. Hauts-de-France

Hauts-de-France is located in the north of France and is known for its beautiful coastline, historic battlefields, and vibrant cities. Some of the popular destinations in Hauts-de-France are Lille, Amiens, and Calais.

3. Île-de-France

Île-de-France is the region surrounding the capital city of Paris. It is known for its iconic landmarks, world-class museums, and beautiful gardens. Some of the popular destinations in Île-de-France are Paris, Versailles, and Fontainebleau.

4. Normandy

Normandy is located in the northwest of France and is known for its beautiful coastline, charming towns, and historic landmarks. Some of the popular destinations in Normandy are Rouen, Caen, and Mont Saint-Michel.

5. Brittany

Brittany is located in the northwest of France and is known for its rugged coastline, charming villages, and delicious seafood. Some of the popular destinations in Brittany are Saint-Malo, Rennes, and Quimper.

6. Pays de la Loire

Pays de la Loire is located in the west of France and is known for its beautiful chateaux, historic towns, and stunning coastline. Some of the popular destinations in Pays de la Loire are Nantes, Angers, and Le Mans.

7. Centre-Val de Loire

Centre-Val de Loire is located in the center of France and is known for its beautiful countryside, historic castles, and world-class wine. Some of the popular destinations in Centre-Val de Loire are Tours, Orleans, and Chartres.

8. Burgundy-Franche-Comté

Burgundy-Franche-Comté is located in the east of France and is known for its world-class wine, delicious cuisine, and historic landmarks. Some of the popular destinations in Burgundy-Franche-Comté are Dijon, Beaune, and Besancon.

9. Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes

Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes is located in the southeast of France and is known for its stunning mountains, beautiful countryside, and vibrant cities. Some of the popular destinations in Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes are Lyon, Grenoble, and Annecy.

10. Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur

Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur is located in the south of France and is known for its beautiful coastline, charming villages, and world-class cuisine. Some of the popular destinations in Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur are Marseille, Nice, and Cannes.

11. Occitanie

Occitanie is located in the south of France and is known for its beautiful coastline, stunning countryside, and historic landmarks. Some of the popular destinations in Occitanie are Toulouse, Montpellier, and Carcassonne.

12. Nouvelle-Aquitaine

Nouvelle-Aquitaine is located in the southwest of France and is known for its beautiful coastline, charming towns, and world-class wine. Some of the popular destinations in Nouvelle-Aquitaine are Bordeaux, La Rochelle, and Biarritz.

13. Corsica

Corsica is an island located in the Mediterranean Sea and is known for its beautiful beaches, rugged mountains, and charming villages. Some of the popular destinations in Corsica are Ajaccio, Bonifacio, and Calvi.

14. Guadeloupe

Guadeloupe is an island located in the Caribbean Sea and is known for its beautiful beaches, lush rainforests, and vibrant culture. Some of the popular destinations in Guadeloupe are Pointe-à-Pitre, Basse-Terre, and Sainte-Anne.

15. Martinique

Martinique is an island located in the Caribbean Sea and is known for its beautiful beaches, delicious cuisine, and vibrant culture. Some of the popular destinations in Martinique are Fort-de-France, Sainte-Anne, and Les Trois-Îlets.

16. French Guiana

French Guiana is located in South America and is known for its lush rainforests, diverse wildlife, and vibrant culture. Some of the popular destinations in French Guiana are Cayenne, Kourou, and Saint-Laurent-du-Maroni.

17. Réunion

Réunion is an island located in the Indian Ocean and is known for its beautiful beaches, stunning mountains, and vibrant culture. Some of the popular destinations in Réunion are Saint-Denis, Saint-Paul, and Saint-Pierre.

18. Mayotte

Mayotte is an island located in the Indian Ocean and is known for its beautiful beaches, diverse wildlife, and vibrant culture. Some of the popular destinations in Mayotte are Mamoudzou, Chirongui, and Bandrele.
